DevOps is a software development methodology and cultural philosophy that combines development (Dev) and IT operations (Ops) teams to accelerate the delivery of high-performance applications. It relies on automation, continuous integration/continuous delivery (CI/CD), and collaboration to enhance efficiency, reduce manual errors, and improve product quality.

Key DevOps Components:
Cultural Philosophy: Breaks down silos between teams, promoting shared responsibility and faster communication.
Automation: Focuses on automating the entire software development life cycle, including testing, infrastructure management, and deployment to minimize human error.
CI/CD (Continuous Integration/Continuous Delivery): Enables rapid, reliable, and frequent software releases.
Key Lifecycle Stages: Planning, coding, building, testing (using automated testing), releasing, deploying, operating, and monitoring for constant feedback
